Basics of Music Theory

Summary

The "Basics of Music Theory" topic consists of six lessons. The first four are optional, since they will be mostly review for students who have previously taken music classes or private lessons. The last two lessons are mandatory, as they explain how to represent basic music theoretical concepts within the computer.

Lessons

  1. Review: The Notes on a Keyboard
    • Vocabulary
      • flat
      • middle C
      • octave
      • register
      • sharp
  2. Review: Scales and Intervals
    • Vocabulary
      • chromatic scale
      • whole step
      • half step
      • interval
      • major scale
      • minor scale
      • tonic
  3. Review: Fundamentals of Rhythm
    • Vocabulary
      • dotted note
      • measure
      • beat
      • rest
      • syncopation
      • triplet
      • whole note, half note, quarter note, eighth note, etc.
  4. Review: Time Signatures and Tempo
    • Vocabulary
      • beats per minute (bpm)
      • tempo
      • time signature (aka meter)
  5. Representing Pitch in the Computer
    • Vocabulary
      • MIDI number
      • pitch class
  6. Representing Rhythm in the Computer
    • Vocabulary
      • absolute time
      • tempo-relative time
